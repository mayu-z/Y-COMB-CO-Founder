Phase 1 — Data Cleaning & Chunking 🧹
Time: 3–4 days
Goal: Turn raw text into clean, well-structured chunks ready for embedding

Step 1 — Clean all text files
For every .txt file across all sources:

Remove HTML tags if any slipped through
Remove excessive whitespace and blank lines
Remove filler words from transcripts — "um", "uh", "you know", "like" when used as filler
Normalize quotes and apostrophes — curly quotes to straight quotes
Verify encoding is UTF-8


Step 2 — Chunk each source differently
This is the most important decision in your whole pipeline. Don't chunk everything the same way.
PG Essays — chunk by idea

Read each essay and split at paragraph breaks where the topic shifts
Target 400–600 words per chunk — enough to hold a complete argument
Keep the essay title and author in every chunk as metadata
One essay might produce 3–8 chunks

YC Blog Posts — chunk by section

Split at heading breaks or major paragraph breaks
Target 300–500 words per chunk
Keep post title, author name, and date in metadata

Startup School Transcripts — chunk by topic segment

Lectures naturally shift topics every 5–10 minutes
Split where the topic clearly changes
Target 500–800 words per chunk — transcripts need more context
Keep lecture title and speaker name in metadata

HN Threads — chunk by post

Each top-level post or high-quality comment is one chunk
Don't split individual comments — they're already short
Keep upvote count, username, thread title in metadata

Company Directory — one row per company

Each company is its own chunk — never split a company across chunks
Combine all columns into one descriptive sentence per company
"Stripe is an Active Fintech company from the USA, batch W09, that builds payment infrastructure for the internet"


Step 3 — Tag every chunk with metadata
Every single chunk needs these fields before it goes anywhere:
source_type:    pg_essay | yc_blog | startup_school | hn | company
title:          name of essay/post/lecture/thread
author:         PG | Michael Seibel | Dalton Caldwell | etc.
topic_tags:     fundraising | pmf | hiring | applying | growth | idea
date:           when it was written/posted
quality_tier:   1 (PG/YC partners) | 2 (Launch HN, companies) | 3 (community)
word_count:     for filtering
Topic tags are important — go through each chunk and manually or programmatically assign 1–3 topic tags. This makes retrieval much more precise later.

Step 4 — Quality filter
Before saving final chunks, remove anything that is:

Under 100 words — too short to be useful
Pure announcements — "YC is now accepting applications for W24"
Duplicate or near-duplicate content
Off-topic — anything not related to startups, building, or YC


Save output as structured JSON
Each chunk saved like this:
{
  "chunk_id": "pg_001",
  "text": "...",
  "source_type": "pg_essay",
  "title": "Do Things That Don't Scale",
  "author": "Paul Graham",
  "topic_tags": ["growth", "early_stage"],
  "quality_tier": 1,
  "word_count": 487
}
Save everything into one file — chunks.json
✅ Phase 1 done when you have a single clean chunks.json with every chunk tagged and quality-filtered.
